Our story

Student-founded,
community-driven.

The Emergency Education Initiative was founded on a simple belief: the knowledge that saves lives shouldn't sit behind a paywall or a hospital waiting room. It belongs in every community.

Our mission

Bridging the gap between emergency and care.

Every year, thousands of people die from cardiac arrests, strokes, and other emergencies that were survivable — not because help wasn't coming, but because no one nearby knew what to do in the minutes before it arrived.

We exist to change that. Our free programs train everyday people to act confidently in the critical window before professional help arrives.

The team

Who we are

EEI was co-founded by two juniors from Northern Valley Regional High School — an EMT and a community advocate — united by a shared belief that emergency education belongs in every community.

Ian Oak
Co-founder & Medical Program Lead
Emergency medicine

Ian Oak is a co-founder and Medical Program Lead of the Emergency Education Initiative. A junior at Northern Valley Regional High School, Ian is a certified EMT who volunteers with his local first aid corps — responding to real emergencies in his community and bringing that firsthand experience directly into every EEI program he leads.

Balancing varsity athletics, school leadership, and his EMT work, Ian has a proven ability to show up when it matters. He's passionate about making emergency education accessible to everyone and is committed to reaching new communities across New Jersey.

Aaron Choi
Co-founder & Executive Director
Research & operations

Aaron Choi is a co-founder and Executive Director of the Emergency Education Initiative. A junior at Northern Valley Regional High School in Old Tappan, NJ, Aaron has spent the past two years working at the intersection of emergency medicine and community service — as an EMS Cadet with the Old Tappan First Aid Corps, responding to real calls alongside certified EMTs, and as a hospital volunteer at St. Joseph's Health in Paterson.

Those experiences gave him a clear picture of what happens when someone nearby doesn't know what to do in a crisis — and a conviction that it doesn't have to be that way. He co-founded EEI to put life-saving knowledge directly into the hands of everyday people, free of charge.

Our history

How we got here

2025
The idea begins
Aaron and Ian begin planning the Emergency Education Initiative, driven by a shared belief that life-saving knowledge should be free and accessible to every community in New Jersey.
2026
EEI launches
The Emergency Education Initiative officially launches with its first free workshops and begins building community partnerships across Bergen County.
Coming soon
Formal nonprofit status
EEI is actively pursuing formal registration to expand fundraising, unlock grant eligibility, and grow our programs across New Jersey.
The future
Reaching every community
Our goal is to bring free emergency education to schools, workplaces, and community centers across all of New Jersey — one workshop at a time.
